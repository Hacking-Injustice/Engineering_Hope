February 28 – March 2, 2025

About

Hacking Injustice is a Boston-based “Hackathon” that seeks to unite local community organizers with students building public interest technology (PIT). The event will take students from 10+ Boston-based universities to build technology in three primary issue areas:

  • Community health
  • Employment
  • Forced displacement

University programs on computer science, statistics, and engineering hardly offer students the opportunity to effectuate change with their technical skills. As such, building PIT can feel isolating, vague, and difficult in the absence of more robust university infrastructure. To fill this gap, we’re running Hacking Injustice to give students the dual opportunity to build impactful technology and meet other students in the process.

Learning Objectives & Outcomes

Learning Objectives Learning Outcomes
Introduce students to resources around PIT Navigate & learn the PIT design paradigm
Produce tech tools and products for local movements Meet other students and learn about resources in the space
Create a longer-term body of students to engage through PIT design Leave with meaningful projects in their portfolios for professional development

Why a “Hackathon”?

Hackathons offer rapid prototyping spaces for students—who otherwise do not have longitudinal exposure to PIT—to learn by building. We expect that this hackathon will serve as a first touchpoint for students interested in public interest tech. By running a Hackathon that centers community voices and leaders, Boston student technologists can:

  • Give back to the local community
  • Explore a movement-centric definition of social impact
  • Employ their skillset in technologically under-resourced sectors
  • Begin meeting other students interested in these intersections

This event is part of a larger, student-run initiative to organize and mobilize student technologists to build PIT.
